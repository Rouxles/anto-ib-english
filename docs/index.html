Introduction In ‘The Pleasure of Books’, a radio address by William Lyon Phelps, figurative and rhetorical devices are used in order to offer comfort to readers, who had just gone through the great depression. Phelps also makes assumptions that listeners enjoy reading books, and have the means and desire to build a collection of them. Through this, Phelps gets his audience hooked into reading and experiencing the unique feeling of reading books. [Read More]
